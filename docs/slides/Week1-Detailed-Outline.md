## CHAPTER 4: SCRUM EVENTS (Slides 23-30)

**Slide 23: Chapter Divider**
- Large number "4"
- Title: "Scrum Events"
- **Visual:** Full-screen gradient background

**Slide 24: The Sprint**
- Title: "The Sprint - The Heartbeat of Scrum"
- Subtitle: "Fixed-length events of one month or less"
- Content:
  - **Sprint Characteristics:**
    - Duration: 1 week to 1 month (consistent length)
    - Starts immediately after previous Sprint
    - Goal: Create a Done Increment
    - Container for all other events
    - Only Product Owner can cancel (rare)
  - **Do's:**
    - ‚úÖ Keep Sprint Goal unchanged
    - ‚úÖ Enable fast feedback
    - ‚úÖ Maintain focus
  - **Don'ts:**
    - ‚ùå Change Sprint Goal mid-Sprint
    - ‚ùå Skip events
    - ‚ùå Add work endangering Sprint Goal
- **Visual:** Two cards (green for do's, red for don'ts)

**Slide 25: Sprint Planning**
- Title: "Sprint Planning"
- Content: Three questions in separate cards:
  - **Why?** ‚ùì (purple) - What value will this Sprint deliver? ‚Üí Sprint Goal
  - **What?** üìã (blue) - What Product Backlog Items will we work on? ‚Üí Selected items
  - **How?** ‚öôÔ∏è (green) - How will we accomplish the work? ‚Üí Sprint Backlog
- Bottom info bar:
  - ‚è±Ô∏è Timebox: Maximum 8 hours for 1-month Sprint
  - üë• Attendees: Entire Scrum Team
- **Visual:** Three cards with distinct colors, info banner at bottom

**Slide 26: Daily Scrum**
- Title: "Daily Scrum"
- Content:
  - **Purpose:** Inspect progress toward Sprint Goal, adapt Sprint Backlog as needed
  - **Format:**
    - 15-minute event for Developers
    - Same time and place every day
    - Reduces complexity
  - **Modern Approach:**
    - Focus on Sprint Goal progress (not 3 questions)
    - Identify impediments
    - Plan next 24 hours of work
  - **Scrum Master Role:** Ensure event happens, coach on staying within timebox
- **Visual:** Clock icon, 15-minute emphasis, daily calendar visual

**Slide 27: Sprint Review**
- Title: "Sprint Review"
- Content:
  - **Purpose:** Inspect Sprint outcome, determine future adaptations
  - **It's NOT just a demo!**
    - Working session with stakeholders
    - Review what was accomplished
    - Discuss what changed in environment
    - Collaborate on what to do next
    - Review timeline, budget, marketplace changes
  - **Outcome:** Revised Product Backlog for next Sprint
  - **Timebox:** Maximum 4 hours for 1-month Sprint
  - **Attendees:** Scrum Team + key stakeholders
- **Visual:** Distinction between "demo" and "review", stakeholder icons

**Slide 28: Sprint Retrospective**
- Title: "Sprint Retrospective"
- Content:
  - **Purpose:** Plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness
  - **Discussion Topics:**
    - What went well?
    - What problems did we encounter?
    - How were problems solved (or not)?
    - What will we commit to improve in next Sprint?
  - **Output:** Actionable improvement items for next Sprint
  - **Timebox:** Maximum 3 hours for 1-month Sprint
  - **Key:** This is where continuous improvement happens!
- **Visual:** Cycle diagram showing retrospective leading to improvements

**Slide 29: Event Timing Summary**
- Title: "Scrum Events Timeline"
- Content:
  - Visual timeline showing all events within a Sprint
  - For 2-week Sprint:
    - Sprint Planning: 4 hours
    - Daily Scrum: 15 min √ó 10 days = 2.5 hours
    - Sprint Review: 2 hours
    - Sprint Retrospective: 1.5 hours
    - Total ceremony time: ~10 hours (6% of Sprint)
- **Visual:** Timeline diagram, percentage calculation

**Slide 30: Events Summary Card**

## CHAPTER 5: SCRUM ARTIFACTS (Slides 31-36)

**Slide 31: Chapter Divider**
- Large number "5"
- Title: "Scrum Artifacts"
- **Visual:** Full-screen gradient background

**Slide 32: The Three Artifacts**
- Title: "Scrum Artifacts & Their Commitments"
- Content: Three cards:
  - **Product Backlog** ‚Üí Committed to: Product Goal
  - **Sprint Backlog** ‚Üí Committed to: Sprint Goal
  - **Increment** ‚Üí Committed to: Definition of Done
- Key insight: "Each artifact contains a commitment to ensure transparency and focus"
- **Visual:** Three connected cards with arrows showing commitment

**Slide 33: Product Backlog**
- Title: "Product Backlog"
- Content:
  - **Definition:** Ordered list of what's needed to improve the product
  - **Characteristics:**
    - Single source of work for Scrum Team
    - Never complete - evolves as product evolves
    - Ordered by value, risk, dependencies
    - Higher order = more refined
  - **Product Owner Accountable For:**
    - Content, ordering, and refinement
    - Ensuring transparency and understanding
  - **Product Goal:** Long-term objective, one at a time
- **Visual:** List visual showing ordered items, decreasing detail

**Slide 34: Sprint Backlog**
- Title: "Sprint Backlog"
- Content:
  - **Definition:** Plan for delivering Increment and achieving Sprint Goal
  - **Composed of:**
    - Sprint Goal (why)
    - Selected Product Backlog Items (what)
    - Plan for delivering them (how)
  - **Ownership:** Created BY and FOR the Developers
  - **Living Document:** Updated throughout Sprint as more is learned
  - **Transparency:** Real-time picture of work Developers plan to accomplish
- **Visual:** Breakdown showing three components

**Slide 35: Increment & Definition of Done**
- Title: "Increment & Definition of Done"
- Content:
  - **Increment:**
    - Concrete stepping stone toward Product Goal
    - Must be usable
    - Multiple Increments can be created within Sprint
    - All Increments additive - work together
    - Must meet Definition of Done
  - **Definition of Done:**
    - Formal description of Increment's quality
    - Shared understanding of what "complete" means
    - If multiple teams: all must have same DoD
    - If organizational standard exists: minimum DoD
  - **Key:** If Increment doesn't meet DoD, it cannot be released
- **Visual:** Checklist showing DoD criteria

**Slide 36: Artifacts Summary**
- Title: "Artifacts at a Glance"
- Content: Quick reference table:
  - Artifact | Purpose | Commitment | Owner
  - Product Backlog | What to build | Product Goal | Product Owner
  - Sprint Backlog | Sprint plan | Sprint Goal | Developers
  - Increment | Done work | Definition of Done | Scrum Team
- **Visual:** Clean table with color coding

- Titles: 36-48px, bold, cyan or white
- Subtitles: 16-20px, muted gray
- Body: 14-16px, white
- Lists: 13-15px, white or light gray
- Emphasis: Bold for key terms

**Layout Principles:**
- Maximum 25% text coverage
- Large amounts of white (dark) space
- Use cards/boxes for grouping content
- Icons/emojis for visual interest
- Consistent padding (20-40px)
- Footer on most slides: "PSM I Training Program ‚Ä¢ Week 1"

**Chapter Dividers:**
- Full screen gradient
- Large number (80px+)
- Chapter title (48px)
- Minimal design, maximum impact

**Best Practices:**
- One main idea per slide
- Use visual hierarchy (size, color, weight)
- Consistent spacing between elements
- Icons enhance, don't distract
- Keep lists to 3-5 items max
