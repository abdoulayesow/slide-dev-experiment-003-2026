## Section 2: AI Fundamentals (0:15-0:40) ‚Äî 8 slides

### Slide 6: What is Generative AI?
- **Simple definition**: "AI that creates new content based on patterns it learned from examples"
- **Analogy**: Like an extremely well-read assistant who can write, analyze, and summarize‚Äîbut doesn't truly "understand" like humans do

### Slide 7: How Does AI "Think"? (Simplified)
- **The pattern recognition approach**:
  - Trained on billions of text examples
  - Predicts what words/phrases likely come next
  - No memory between conversations (unless told to remember)
  - No access to your files (unless you share them)

## Section 4: CRAFT Framework (1:10-1:35) ‚Äî 6 slides + exercise

### Slide 17: The Problem with Most Prompts
- **Common mistakes participants make**:
  - Too vague: "Write an email about [topic]"
  - Missing context: AI has to guess your intent
  - Wrong format: Get a paragraph when you needed bullets
  - No audience consideration: Too technical or too simple
- **The result**: Mediocre outputs that require extensive editing

### Slide 18: Introducing the CRAFT Framework (Universal)
- **Large, memorable acronym display**:
  - **C** - Context
  - **R** - Role
  - **A** - Action
  - **F** - Format
  - **T** - Target audience
- **Note**: "This framework works across ALL industries‚Äîtax, real estate, legal, consulting, you name it"
- **Visual**: Each letter on separate line with icon

### Slide 19: CRAFT Breakdown - Context
- **C = Context**: "What's the situation?"
- **Examples across industries**:
  - Tax: "I'm preparing for a client meeting about year-end tax planning..."
  - Real Estate: "A first-time buyer just asked about closing costs..."
  - Legal: "I need to explain discovery process to a new client..."
  - Consulting: "We're proposing a digital transformation project..."
- **Why it matters**: Gives AI the background to provide relevant advice
- **Visual**: Speech bubble with context example

### Slide 20: CRAFT Breakdown - Role & Action
- **R = Role**: "What expert should AI be?"
  - "Act as an experienced [tax advisor/real estate agent/attorney/consultant]..."
  - "You're a [CPA/broker/paralegal/business advisor] explaining to..."
- **A = Action**: "What specifically should AI do?"
  - "Draft a letter that..."
  - "Summarize the key points of..."
  - "Create a checklist for..."
- **Visual**: Two-column layout with examples

### Slide 21: CRAFT Breakdown - Format & Target
- **F = Format**: "How should the output look?"
  - "Bullet points with brief explanations"
  - "Professional email (300 words max)"
  - "Table comparing options"
- **T = Target audience**: "Who's receiving this?"
  - "Small business owner with limited [tax/legal/technical] knowledge"
  - "Experienced investor familiar with market analysis"
  - "C-suite executive needing strategic overview"
- **Visual**: Two-column layout with examples

### Slide 22: CRAFT in Action - Complete Example
- **Bad prompt**: "Write about [topic]"
- **CRAFT prompt** (using tax example, but note universality):
  - **C**: "I'm preparing a Q4 email to remind clients about estimated tax deadlines"
  - **R**: "Act as a helpful tax advisor"
  - **A**: "Draft an email explaining the January 15 deadline and consequences of missing it"
  - **F**: "Professional but friendly tone, 200-250 words"
  - **T**: "Small business owners who handle their own estimated payments"
- **Note on slide**: "This same structure works for any professional communication‚Äîjust adapt the specifics to your industry"
- **Visual**: Side-by-side comparison showing before/after quality

## Section 6: Tool Comparison (2:20-2:45) ‚Äî 7 slides

### Slide 28: The AI Tool Landscape

### Slide 29: ChatGPT Overview
- **Strengths**:
  - Most widely used (largest community)
  - Excellent for drafting and creative tasks
  - Extensive plugin ecosystem
  - Strong general knowledge
- **Limitations**:
  - Free tier has usage caps
  - Can be overly verbose
  - Data privacy concerns on free tier
- **Best for**: Versatile drafting, brainstorming, general use across industries
- **Pricing**: Free tier / $20/mo Plus / Enterprise pricing varies

### Slide 30: Claude Overview
- **Strengths**:
  - Handles extremely long documents (200K tokens = ~500 pages)
  - More "careful" and less prone to hallucinations
  - Strong analytical reasoning
  - Does NOT train on user data by default
- **Limitations**:
  - Smaller user community than ChatGPT
  - Sometimes overly cautious/verbose
- **Best for**: Contract review, compliance-sensitive work, long document analysis
- **Pricing**: Free tier / $20/mo Pro

### Slide 31: Microsoft Copilot Overview
- **Strengths**:
  - Deep integration with Office 365 (Word, Excel, Outlook)
  - Works within your existing workflow
  - Inherits your organization's security posture
  - No context switching between tools
- **Limitations**:
  - Requires Microsoft 365 subscription
  - Less powerful for complex reasoning vs. ChatGPT/Claude
- **Best for**: If you're already in the Microsoft ecosystem
- **Pricing**: Free tier / $30/mo with M365 / Enterprise included in M365 Copilot

### Slide 32: Perplexity AI Overview
- **Strengths**:
  - Provides citations and sources for claims
  - Excellent for research requiring verification
  - Good for current events (searches web in real-time)
  - Clean, focused interface
- **Limitations**:
  - Limited document processing
  - Smaller knowledge base than competitors
  - Relies on publicly available info only
- **Best for**: Research, regulatory updates, verifiable information across industries
- **Pricing**: Free tier / $20/mo Pro

## Section 8: Ethics, Privacy & Professional Standards (3:15-3:40) ‚Äî 8 slides

### Slide 39: Why This Matters for All Professionals
- **You're bound by professional standards**:
  - Industry-specific regulations
  - Professional codes of conduct
  - Client confidentiality obligations
  - Data protection laws
- **The stakes**: License, reputation, client trust, legal liability
- **Visual**: Shield icon with "Professional Standards" text

### Slide 40: The Universal Data Privacy Rules
**NEVER input into consumer AI tools**:
1. ‚ùå Social Security Numbers or national IDs
2. ‚ùå Bank account or payment card numbers
3. ‚ùå Full client names paired with financial/sensitive data
4. ‚ùå Tax IDs, license numbers, case numbers
5. ‚ùå Exact client addresses
6. ‚ùå Specific dollar amounts tied to identifiable clients
7. ‚ùå Any information that could identify a specific client matter
- **Visual**: Red "prohibited" icons for each

### Slide 41: What IS Safe to Use
**‚úÖ Safe for AI assistance**:
- Template communications (identifying info removed)
- General research questions
- Explaining concepts in plain language
- Creating checklists and procedures
- Analyzing anonymized data
- Drafting generic documents
- Strategic planning (general scenarios)
- **Key**: Remove all personally identifiable information first

### Slide 42: The Anonymization Technique
- **How to anonymize effectively**:
  - Replace names: "Client A", "Smith Family", "Company XYZ"
  - Round numbers: "$250,000" becomes "approximately $250K"
  - Remove specifics: "a manufacturing client" not "ABC Manufacturing Inc."
  - Genericize situations: "a small business owner" not your actual client
- **Example transformation** shown on slide
- **Works across all industries**
- **Visual**: Before/after comparison

### Slide 43: Enterprise vs. Consumer AI Tools
**Consumer tools (ChatGPT free, Claude free)**:
- May use conversations for training
- Limited compliance features
- Shared infrastructure
- Basic privacy controls

**Enterprise tools (ChatGPT Enterprise, Teams, Business tier)**:
- SOC 2 certification
- No training on your data
- Admin controls and audit logs
- Industry compliance features (BAA for HIPAA, etc.)
- AES-256 encryption

**Recommendation**: Consult with IT/compliance before using with any client data

### Slide 44: Professional Responsibility Checklist
- **Questions to ask before using AI**:
  - Am I relying on AI outputs without verification?
  - Would I be comfortable disclosing this AI use to my client?
  - Have I maintained sufficient professional skepticism?
  - Am I still exercising professional judgment?
  - Does this comply with my industry's standards?
- **The standard**: AI is a tool, YOU are the professional
- **Visual**: Checklist format

### Slide 45: Industry-Specific Considerations
- **Tax/Accounting**: IRS Circular 230, AICPA Code, Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, IRS Section 7216
- **Legal**: Attorney-client privilege, ethics rules, bar regulations
- **Real Estate**: License requirements, fair housing laws, data protection
- **Financial Services**: SEC regulations, FINRA rules, fiduciary duties
- **Healthcare**: HIPAA compliance, patient privacy
- **General**: GDPR, state privacy laws, professional codes
